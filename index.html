    Frequently Asked Questions

    dawg.city extracts the high-resolution thumbnail from any YouTube URL and runs it through Sightengine's GenAI detection model. The model returns a probability score indicating how likely the content is AI-generated or synthetic. We return a binary verdict — Fake or Real — based on a 50% confidence threshold.
    No AI detector is 100% accurate. dawg.city is a probabilistic tool — it can have false positives (real videos flagged as fake) and false negatives (AI videos marked as real), especially as generation technology improves. Use it as a helpful indicator, not a definitive verdict.
    Currently dawg.city supports YouTube videos, YouTube Shorts, and YouTube Live streams. Support for X (Twitter), TikTok, and direct video uploads is planned for future paid tiers.
